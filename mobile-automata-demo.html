About Mobile Automata

Mobile automata, explored by Stephen Wolfram in "A New Kind of Science," are computational systems where one or more active cells move along a line of cells, updating them according to specific rules. Unlike cellular automata where all cells update simultaneously, mobile automata update only the active cells and their immediate neighbors.

The behavior can range from simple repetitive motion to complex sweeping patterns. Some rules cause the active cell to move in predictable cycles, while others create intricate back-and-forth movements that cover increasingly larger areas. Advanced variants allow cells to split or disappear, creating even richer dynamics.
